HR-Analytics-Attrition-Analysis

HR Analytics project focused on analyzing employee attrition using Power BI. Includes KPIs, dashboards, and insights to support employee retention strategies.

Project Overview
- Analyzes employee attrition (resignations) using the IBM HR dataset
- Identifies patterns, trends, and key factors influencing attrition
- Provides actionable insights to improve employee retention

Key Business Questions
- What is the overall attrition rate?
- Which age group has the highest attrition?
- How do salary and tenure impact attrition?
- Which departments are most affected?

KPIs Tracked
- Attrition Rate (%)
- Average Monthly Salary
- Attrition by Department
- Attrition by Age Group
- Attrition by Gender

Key Insights
- The Sales department has the highest attrition (20%+), indicating potentially high-pressure roles
- Employees aged 25–34 contribute to the majority of attrition, highlighting mid-career challenges
- The highest attrition is observed among Life Sciences and Medical graduates
- The average salary is 6.5K, with higher attrition observed in lower salary bands

Business Impact
- Helps identify high-risk employee segments
- Supports the development of retention strategies for mid-career professionals
- Highlights departments with high attrition, such as Sales and R&D
- Assists in improving job satisfaction and employee engagement initiatives
